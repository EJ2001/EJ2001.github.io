The challenge

Headrush Arena is a multiplayer Meta Quest arena shooter built on Coherence's Cloud Rooms architecture. The challenge was shipping a coherent, fun shooter with convincing bot AI, multiple game modes, and a full progression system in a constrained VR environment where performance headroom is tight and latency in networked state has to be handled properly, not glossed over.

What I built & why

  • Bot AI system built on Behaviour Trees with a communication layer where bots report state to the AI Director through events including casualty reporting and requests for reinforcement. The Director uses this to redistribute pressure and keep encounters feeling coordinated rather than chaotic.
  • Host authority networking with defensive design. Commands over Coherence are single packets with no delivery guarantee, so anything that needs to be correct for late joiners or survive packet loss uses synced state rather than one off messages. Built explicit recovery paths for failure modes that actually happen in practice, including a map load watchdog that kicks stragglers rather than hanging the whole lobby, and separate handling for a lobby owner leaving versus a slower authority migration.
  • Spawn intensity system keeping bot pressure engaging and properly paced without dead zones between encounters.
  • Global and local announcer service tracking killstreaks and match events. Major moments broadcast to all players, personal events fire locally only, giving players both competitive accomplishment and shared social moments.
  • Shipped PVE Survival, TDM and FFA modes, a configurable upgrade system, and a full player feedback layer including kill feed, hitmarkers and nameplates.

The result

A shipped multiplayer VR shooter with stable networked gameplay, a coordinated bot AI system that keeps encounters feeling fair and fun, and enough game modes and progression to give players a reason to keep coming back.

The project

Campus XR is an immersive VR education platform for schools on Meta Quest. Across the project I contributed to a range of gameplay and AI systems spanning multiple experiences, from AI debate characters to gamified learning environments.

What I worked on

  • AI debate system where students speak to historical AI characters using Whisper for voice input. An AI judge evaluates the student's argument against the character's in real time, with scored results shown on a shared leaderboard.
  • Utility AI for interactive animals with player bonding behaviours. Animals evaluate behaviours via weighted considerations and respond naturally to student presence.
  • Range of gamified learning experiences including a multiplayer quiz arena and educational minigames across the platform.
  • Tutorial and onboarding system built to recognise whether the user is a teacher or student and serve the appropriate module flow, designed for people using VR for the first time with no assumed prior experience.

The result

A deployed education VR platform in active use in schools globally, spanning AI debate characters with live voice scoring, gamified learning environments, and onboarding systems that adapt to teachers and students differently.
